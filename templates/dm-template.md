# Short-Form DM Templates — OSS Sponsorship Outreach

Short messages for channels where long copy gets you muted: LinkedIn, X/Twitter,
Discord/Slack, 微信, and public comment threads (V2EX/掘金). Every template is
under ~150 words, conversational, with a single soft CTA — usually "move to
email/call" or "open to a quick chat?".

## Channel etiquette — read before you send

- **Never mass-blast.** Personalization isn't optional decoration on these
  channels — it's the difference between a reply and a block. If you can't
  reference one specific thing about the recipient, don't send.
- **Respect rate limits and platform rules.** LinkedIn restricts connection
  requests to people you don't know; X/Twitter DMs require the recipient to
  follow you or have open DMs; Discord/Slack servers have their own rules and
  mods who ban unsolicited DMs on sight. 微信 can flag or shadow-restrict
  accounts that send too many similar messages to non-contacts.
- **Move off-channel fast.** The job of a DM is to earn the right to continue
  the conversation in email or a call, where you can share a
  [rate card](rate-card.md) and [media kit](media-kit.md) without blowing up a
  150-word limit.
- **One ask, soft.** "Open to a 15-min chat?" not "Here are 6 tiers and a logo
  spec." Full email sequences live in [cold-email.md](cold-email.md).
- **No price quotes in a DM.** If a number must come up, label it
  "estimate / reference only" and point to the rate card. Never present an
  estimate as a quote.
- **Public threads (V2EX/掘金) are value-first, never cold asks.** See #5.

## 1. LinkedIn DM

### 1a. Connection note (300-char limit — be tight)

> Hi [first-name] — I maintain [your-repo] ([star-count]★), and noticed
> [their-product] sits right in our users' workflow at [specific-overlap].
> Not pitching anything — would value connecting with DevRel folks building
> for the same devs. Open to a quick chat later?

### 1b. Follow-up after they accept (1–2 days later)

> Thanks for connecting, [first-name]. Quick context: [your-repo] reaches
> ~[monthly-metric] monthly [visitors/downloads] at the tool-selection moment,
> and [their-product] is already in that flow. I keep a sponsorship rate card
> (estimate / reference only) and would rather walk you through it on a
> 15-min call than dump it here. Open to a slot this or next week? Here's my
> calendar: [scheduling-link].

## 2. X / Twitter DM

> Hey [handle] — big fan of [their-product], use it for [specific thing].
> I maintain [your-repo] ([star-count]★, ~[monthly-metric] monthly
> [visitors/downloads]). [their-product] keeps showing up in how people use
> it — [one concrete overlap]. Wondering if a README/docs sponsorship is worth
> a 15-min chat? No deck, no pressure. Happy to send a rate card
> (estimate only) to your email if easier.

> **Note:** X DMs only work if they follow you or have DMs open. If closed,
> reply to one of their tweets with a public, value-first one-liner ("loved
> the [feature] post — [your-repo] leans on it for [x]") and let them opt in.

## 3. Discord / Slack DM to a DevRel in a community server

> Hey [first-name] — saw you're on DevRel at [their-company] in [server-name].
> I maintain [your-repo] ([star-count]★); a chunk of this server runs it on
> [their-product] ([one concrete overlap]). Not posting this in-channel to
> avoid noise — but if sponsorship of the README/docs is ever on your radar,
> I've got a rate card (estimate only) and would take a 15-min call. Totally
> fine if not. Either way, thanks for [their-product].

> **Server etiquette:** never cold-DM a member you haven't interacted with in
> channel first. Help answer a question or two in-channel over a few days, *then*
> DM. Mods ban unsolicited commercial DMs; a warm intro is the only safe path.

## 4. 微信私信 — 国内 AI-API / 云服务 / 开发者工具 BD（中文）

> [first-name] 你好，冒昧打扰。我是 [your-repo]（[star-count]★）的维护者，
> [一句话项目用途]，月均 [月访问量 / 下载量]。注意到 [their-product] 在
> [your-repo] 的使用场景里经常出现——[一处具体交集]。想问下 README／文档站
> 赞助合作是否有机会聊聊？我有一份报价参考表（**仅供参考，非正式报价**），
> 方便的话约 15 分钟电话过一遍，不合适直说无妨。需要的话我把资料发你邮箱。
> 感谢 [their-product]，我们在 [具体用途] 用到。

> **国内 BD 注意：**
> - 国内 1k-star 量级仓库的常见月度区间约 **¥500–¥5,000**（estimate，详见
>   [rate-card.md](rate-card.md) 的 RMB 版），但实际取决于流量与受众契合度，
>   star 数只是参考。
> - 微信对非好友或陌生账号的相似文案群发会风控/限制，**逐条手改、低频发送**。
> - 国内收款常走对公转账或个人收款码；GitHub Sponsors 不支持中国大陆
>   （香港/澳门特区可），见 [../data/platforms.yml](../data/platforms.yml)。
> - 先在社区（V2EX/掘金/技术群）建立可见度，再私信，转化率与安全度都更高。

## 5. V2EX / 掘金 公开评论式触达 — value-first, not spammy

> 这类是**公开回复**而非私信，核心是先给价值、不带直接推销。模板用于在你
> 维护的项目相关帖子下，或对方公司/产品相关帖子下，以"项目维护者"身份自然
> 出现，让感兴趣的人主动来问。

**V2EX 主题/评论示例（分享项目动态时顺带留 BD 口子）：**

> 分享一下 [your-repo] 最近发了 [version]：[一句新功能亮点]。目前
> [star-count]★、月均 [月访问量]，[their-product] / [另一家] 的用户是我们
> 增长最快的一群。如果贵司在做开发者侧投放，README／文档站赞助位我们开放
> 了一部分（报价参考表见 [rate-card-link]，**仅供参考**）。有想法的欢迎
> 节点内私信，或邮件 [your-email]。

**掘金评论示例（在对方产品相关文章下，以维护者身份补充价值）：**

> 维护 [your-repo] 顺手补充一句：文中提到的 [feature]，我们在
> [your-repo] 里就是配合 [their-product] 的 [x] 实现的，[一句技术细节]。
> 如果有团队在评估这条链路，README／文档站我们留了赞助位（报价仅供参考），
> 详情见 [rate-card-link]，或私信我。

> **公开帖守则：**
> - **永远先给技术价值**（新版本、细节、踩坑），赞助信息作为末尾一句"顺便
>   提一下"，绝不做主帖主题。主帖纯广告会被踩/删/封号。
> - 留一个**可追踪的链接**（rate-card 短链 + 邮箱），让感兴趣的人主动来，
>   而非群发。
> - 同一论坛短期内不要重复发相似 BD 帖，按平台"冷却期"走（V2EX 节点各有
>   规则，掘金对营销内容审核较严）。

## 6. Reply to interest — move to email/call

> Glad it resonates, [first-name]. Easiest if I send the full picture to your
> email — a [rate card](rate-card.md) (estimate / reference only) + a short
> [media kit](media-kit.md) with audience numbers. What's the best address?
> Or if you'd rather just talk, here's a 15-min slot: [scheduling-link].
> Either works.

> **中文版：**
> [first-name] 好，正好对上了。最方便的是我把完整资料发你邮箱——
> [报价参考表]（仅供参考）+ 一份简版 [媒体包]（含受众数据）。邮箱是哪个？
> 或直接聊也行，这是 15 分钟的时段：[scheduling-link]。

## 7. Reply to no — graceful

> No worries at all, [first-name] — appreciate the reply. I'll leave it there.
> If budgets open up later, the [rate card](rate-card.md) and my calendar
> ([scheduling-link]) don't move. And if a paid slot's off the table but
> performance-based (affiliate) ever makes sense, I'm open to that too.
> Thanks for [their-product].

> **中文版：**
> 没问题 [first-name]，感谢回复，那就不打扰了。后续若预算有变化，我的
> [报价参考表] 和日历（[scheduling-link]）都在。若付费位暂时不方便、但
> 按转化付费（affiliate）合适，也可以聊。感谢 [their-product]。

## Quick reference — which channel for whom

| Recipient | Best first channel | Why |
|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| DevRel / developer-marketing | LinkedIn DM → email | Professional norm; their role is literally "reach devs" |
| Indie founder / growth lead | X/Twitter DM or reply | They live in public; warm public reply earns the DM |
| Engineer at a company using your project | In-channel interaction → Discord/Slack DM | Cold DMs get banned; warm in-channel help first |
| 国内云服务 / AI-API / 开发者工具 BD | 微信私信（先在社区建立可见度） | 国内商务默认微信；先可见后私信 |
| Broad dev audience (no specific contact) | V2EX / 掘金 value-first public post | Public, searchable, lets them opt in |

> For the full email sequence (opener + 2 follow-ups + YES/NO replies + close-out),
> see [cold-email.md](cold-email.md).
